AI in Traffic Management: Building VIDES

How we architected a real-time Highway Traffic Management System (HTMS) to detect accidents in milliseconds using YOLOv8 and DeepSORT.

At Arya Omnitalk, I had the opportunity to work on the Highway Traffic Management System (HTMS), specifically the VIDES module. The goal was ambitious: automate the detection of traffic violations and accidents on high-speed expressways without a human in the loop.

A car moving at 120 km/h covers 33 meters per second. That means our system had to capture, process, and analyze a frame in **under 200ms** to successfully trigger an alert before the vehicle exited the camera's field of view.

1. The Need for Speed (and Accuracy)

Detecting a car is easy. Detecting a car in heavy rain, at night, while it's hidden behind a truck? That's hard.

Core Problems

  • Occlusion: Large trucks often hide smaller cars entirely for seconds at a time.
  • Motion Blur: At 120km/h, standard shutter speeds blur license plates beyond recognition.
  • Ghosting: Reflections on wet roads can be mistaken for vehicles.

2. The VIDES Pipeline

3. YOLO + DeepSORT: The Golden Pair

We used **YOLOv8** for detection because of its balance between speed and accuracy (mAP). But detection alone isn't enough; you can't calculate speed without tracking the *same* vehicle across frames.

Enter **DeepSORT** (Simple Online and Realtime Tracking with a Deep Association Metric). It assigns a unique ID to each car.

tracker.py 
def calculate_speed(track_id, current_pos, fps):
    # Retrieve previous position from history
    prev_pos = history[track_id][-1]
    
    # Pixel to Meter conversion (Calibrated per camera)
    distance_pixels = euclidean_dist(current_pos, prev_pos)
    distance_meters = distance_pixels * PIXEL_METER_RATIO
    
    # Speed = Distance / Time
    time_sec = 1 / fps
    speed_mps = distance_meters / time_sec
    speed_kmh = speed_mps * 3.6
    
    return speed_kmh

4. Edge Optimization

Running this on a full server is expensive. We optimized the model to run on **NVIDIA Jetson Xavier** modules mounted directly on the gantries.

  • TensorRT: We converted the PyTorch weights to TensorRT engines, achieving a 4x speedup.
  • FP16 Quantization: Reduced precision from 32-bit to 16-bit floating point with negligible accuracy loss.

5. Real World Impact

The system is now live on major expressways. Not only has it automated over 5,000 fines per month, but more importantly, the **Accident Detection Module** alerts highway patrol within 15 seconds of a vehicle stopping in a high-speed lane, potentially saving lives.
